## Dependency notes

- 001 establishes stable product/non-goal language, tracker states, and the
  repository-owned workflow contract used by every later plan.
- 002 is the operational foundation. Recipes, context linkage, UX, and
  comparisons must consume one event journal rather than create parallel logs.
- 003 is the first execution tracer bullet. Do not add more recipes until its
  worktree, gate, verification, and handoff behavior has been reviewed in use.
- 004 can proceed alongside 003 after 002. It resolves the current split between
  Karl metadata and Ivo context content.
- 005 deliberately follows 002 because terminal output is a projection of the
  journal, not a second source of truth.
- 006 follows 004 so comparisons can prove they used identical context.

## Symphony concepts adopted

The plans selectively borrow from the OpenAI Symphony service specification:

- repository-owned workflow policy;
- explicit work item, attempt, workspace, and live-session concepts;
- isolated persistent workspaces;
- one authority for lifecycle transitions;
- typed failure/terminal reasons, bounded retries, and reconciliation;
- structured, operator-visible observability;
- successful execution may hand off to human review rather than imply Done.

Reference: <https://github.com/openai/symphony/blob/main/SPEC.md>

## Considered and rejected for this sequence

- **Long-running polling daemon**: premature; first prove one manually invoked,
  recoverable recipe and its operating contract.
- **General DAG/workflow engine**: conflicts with Karl's slim product thesis;
  one typed recipe is enough to test the architecture.
- **Automatic workspace cleanup**: rejected after the 2026-07-11 recovery
  incident; failed and completed scratch worktrees remain until explicit cleanup.
- **Full split-pane TUI / Ink dependency**: most value should come from concise
  live summaries plus durable inspection first.
- **Skill marketplace, ratings, and team registry**: ecosystem work before the
  runtime is trustworthy would multiply compatibility and support obligations.
- **Automatic model learning or global capability rankings**: opaque and hard to
  reproduce; explicit comparisons are the smaller, inspectable primitive.
- **Enterprise policy suite, editor integrations, KFL, and daemon context
  watching**: interesting later, but not part of the current product-defining
  sequence.
